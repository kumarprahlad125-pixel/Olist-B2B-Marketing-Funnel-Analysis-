# Olist-B2B Marketing Funnel Analysis

## Overview

This project studies Olist's marketing funnel — how leads are generated and which of those leads turn into actual paying customers (sellers) on the platform.

The analysis covers 8,000 marketing qualified leads and 842 closed deals. SQL was used to explore the data, and Power BI was used to build a dashboard summarizing the results.


## Dataset

Source-KAGGLE 

Two tables: marketing_qualified_leads (8,000 records) and closed_deals (842 records), linked by a shared mql_id

Time period: 2017-2018

Overall conversion rate from lead to closed deal: about 10.5% 


## Tools Used

MySQL Workbench

SQL

Power BI


## Business Problem

Olist's marketing and sales teams want clear answers to a few key questions:

Which business segments generated the highest number of closed deals?

Which lead acquisition channels generated the highest number of qualified leads?

Which business types generated the highest declared monthly revenue?

What is the average monthly revenue generated by each business segment?

Which lead acquisition channels resulted in the highest number of closed deals?



## Methodology

Clean and organize the data in Ms excel .

Loaded the leads and deals data into a MySQL database.

Wrote SQL queries to analyze leads and deals by channel, business segment, and business type.

Joined the two tables to connect each lead source to its actual sales outcome.

Built a Power BI dashboard to present the results clearly.

Summarized the results into a short set of findings and recommendations.

## Key Findings

Home decor is the top business segment by number of closed deals (105), followed by health & beauty (93) and car accessories (77).

Organic search leads on every major metric — most leads, most closed deals, and most revenue.

Manufacturers generate far more total revenue ($50.8M) than resellers ($10.3M), even though resellers close more individual deals.

Construction tools/house & garden and phone & mobile segments have relatively few closed deals, but each deal is worth significantly more on average — $734,710 and $615,385 respectively.

Several segments earn well above the overall average of $73,378 per deal, making them strong candidates for extra investment despite lower deal volume.

Channels such as display, referral, and other minor sources generate little to no measurable revenue, even where some conversion occurs.


## Key Business Insight

Organic search is the strongest channel in the funnel by a clear margin. It generates the most leads (2,296 of 8,000, about 29% of the total), converts the highest share of them into closed deals (271 deals, close to a 12% conversion rate), and produces the most revenue by far — around $51.4 million in declared monthly revenue, roughly 84% of all revenue tied to a known channel. The average deal from organic search is worth about $189,764, compared to about $47,021 from paid search. This shows that organic search does not just bring in more leads — it brings in higher-quality leads that are more likely to become valuable customers.


## Business Recommendations


Increase investment in organic search  as it is the strongest-performing channel by lead quality and revenue.

Continue supporting paid search, but review spend on social, email, and display channels, which show weak conversion into revenue.

Prioritize sales attention on high-value segments such as construction tools/house & garden and phone & mobile, since each deal in these segments carries significant value.

Investigate why resellers close more deals while manufacturers generate substantially more revenue, and consider a different approach to onboarding or pricing for manufacturer-type sellers.

Track conversion rate by channel on an ongoing basis, not just lead volume, since a high number of leads does not always translate into revenue.
